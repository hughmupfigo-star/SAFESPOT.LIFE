How SAFE works

Free for young people. Funded by the people who need the data.

SAFE isn't a charity asking for your sympathy — we're building something different. The advice stays free and unconditional. The Story Intelligence report pays for it. That's how a service like this survives without ever becoming another gatekeeper.

The free side

Always free. No catch.

Every guide, every tool, every answer — free and unconditional. No sign-up, no means test, no waiting list. If you're young and you need it, it's yours.

The paid side

The monthly report.

Anonymised pattern analysis of what young people are actually facing — bought by the DfE, councils, NGOs, researchers and journalists who need to understand it before it becomes a headline.

The principle

Independence, not charity.

The paid side funds the free side. So SAFE never depends on the institutions it routes young people around — and never has to soften the truth to keep a funder happy.

The guardrail

We never sell anything that could identify a young person. Aggregated patterns only, always anonymised. That isn't just our ethics — it's the entire point. The data has value because the trust behind it is real.

SAFE Tools — 003

Translate this letter.

Paste any official letter you've been scared to open — a Section 21 from your landlord, a Universal Credit decision, a college exclusion, a debt collector, a court summons. We'll explain it in plain English, pull out the dates, tell you what to do, and who to call for free help. Nothing is stored.

Example output

Letter type: Section 21 eviction notice

What this is saying: Your landlord is asking you to leave by 25 August. They cannot lawfully evict you before that date and must apply to court if you don't leave.

Watch out for: Two months' notice is the legal minimum. Don't leave the property before this date.
