Course Modules

(01)

You Deserve Support

Asking for help is not weakness, drama, attention-seeking, or being a burden — even when an adult in your life has trained you to think it is. This module lays the foundation: what mental health actually is, how to tell the difference between a hard week and a real problem, and why the voice that says 'other people have it worse' is the same voice that keeps people from getting better.

(02)

Reading Your Own Weather

Most people only notice their mental health once it's already a storm. This module teaches you to spot the conditions earlier — sleep, appetite, focus, the kind of music you reach for, who you stop replying to, the tone of your inner monologue. Interoception in plain language. A daily 30-second check-in you can actually keep up with. The early-warning signs that mean: do something now, before this gets bigger.

(03)

Tools for the Hard Moments

The toolkit for when it hits hardest. Box breathing. The 5-4-3-2-1 grounding sequence. Cold water on your face. The physiological sigh. Why moving your body for 90 seconds resets your nervous system. What to do when a panic attack starts. What to do when you can't get out of bed. Concrete, evidence-based, no incense required.

(04)

Building Your Support Web

School counsellors are stretched thin and waitlists are long — this module is about everything else. Free crisis text lines (text HOME to 741741 in the US, SHOUT to 85258 in the UK). 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Sliding-scale therapy via Open Path Collective. Affordable apps that aren't scams. How to find one trusted adult outside your family. How to ask a friend's parent for help without it being weird. The web you build now is the one that catches you later.

(05)

Being There for a Friend

Half the messages young people get about a friend's mental health come from TikTok and are wrong. This module: what to actually say when a friend tells you they're struggling. How to ask about suicide without making it worse (you won't — the research is clear). When and how to tell a trusted adult, even if your friend asked you not to. How to support someone without absorbing their weight. Why caring for them and protecting yourself are not opposites.

(06)

Staying Steady

Mental health is a practice, not a project. This final module is about what makes the toolkit stick: sleep that isn't optional, movement you don't dread, food that isn't a punishment, a routine that gives shape to the week without becoming a prison. How to recover after a setback without spiralling about the setback. Tracking what works. Letting go of what doesn't. Building a life you don't need to escape from.

About this course

This course was written for the generation that grew up being told their feelings were valid and then discovered the appointment book was empty. School counsellors have impossible caseloads. State-funded youth mental health programs have been cut. Therapy on private insurance is gatekept by deductibles. The waitlists are months. And the kids still need to make it through Tuesday.

This course is the toolkit you can keep in your pocket while you wait for the system to fix itself. None of it replaces a real human professional. All of it is what a real human professional would teach you to do on your own, between sessions, in the moments when nobody else can be there. Free, self-paced, evidence-based, no log-in.
