Questions

How does this actually work?

Why is SAFE doing this? What's the actual point? ▼
Real talk: we're building an alternative. Energy has to go somewhere. Either into something that builds you up or something that tears you down. We've seen what happens when young people don't have a legitimate way to make money, build identity, and feel like they matter. They find other ways. Gangs. Drugs. Violence. Things that destroy them and everyone around them. This program exists because we believe you're capable of creating something real, something that matters, something that can make you money without destroying your future. It's not charity. It's investment in you because we genuinely believe you're worth it.
Why is this different from Etsy or eBay or any other selling platform? ▼
Good question. Etsy and eBay exist to make money for Etsy and eBay. They take a cut of every sale. They're platforms designed to extract value. We're the opposite. We exist to get out of your way. You make something, you put it on our platform, you keep all the money. We make money through donations and partnerships—not by charging you. But here's the bigger difference: we actually know you. We're not a faceless algorithm. If you submit something, a real person reviews it. If you need help, we help. If you have questions, we answer them. And we're not just about transactions—we're about building community with you, about knowing you as a creator, about supporting your growth. Etsy wants your money. We want your future.
How does creativity keep young people safe? Isn't this just a feel-good project? ▼
It's not feel-good. It's neuroscience. When your brain is engaged in creating something, it's in a different state. You're problem-solving. You're expressing. You're building something from nothing. That takes energy. The same energy that would go into destructive paths. Plus, when you're building something you're proud of, when you're earning real money from it, when you're getting recognition for your work—your identity shifts. You start seeing yourself as a creator, not as a statistic. You start thinking about the future differently. And practically speaking: if you're making £100 a month from selling your art, you're less vulnerable to someone offering you £50 to move product for them. You've got legitimate income. You've got hope. You've got a reason to stay safe.
Why should I trust SAFE with this? How do I know you're not just exploiting young people like everyone else? ▼
You shouldn't trust us just because we say so. Trust is earned. Look at what we do: we keep 100% of proceeds with you. We don't take commissions. We don't sell your data. We don't use you to build investor returns. Our founder grew up poor, survived homelessness, and built SAFE because the systems that were supposed to protect him failed him. This program isn't theoretical for us. It's personal. It comes from understanding exactly what young people are up against. But don't trust our words—trust the structure. We're transparent. We're accountable. And if we ever try to take a cut or change the deal, you'll know immediately because the money in your account will be different.
What happens if I make real money from this? Will it affect my benefits or get me in trouble? ▼
This is important and complicated. Money from selling your own products is legitimate income, but depending on what benefits you or your family receive, it might affect eligibility. We're not accountants and we're not lawyers. What we are is honest: talk to your parents or guardians about this. Ask them to contact a benefits advisor or look up the rules for your specific situation. The money is yours to keep—we just want you to know the full picture before you start. And actually, one of the reasons we're running this program is to help young people move away from reliance on benefits by building real economic opportunity. That's the goal.
If creativity and income keep me safe, why do I also need SAFE's courses and programs? ▼
Because creativity alone isn't enough. You can be the best artist in the world and still be trapped in cycles that destroy you—toxic relationships, family trauma, systems designed to control you. The courses teach you the architecture of those systems so you can see them clearly. They teach you how to protect your energy, your sexuality, your mind, your money. The products program gives you economic opportunity. The hub gives you community. The courses give you understanding. Together, they build a foundation. Separately, they're incomplete. We need all of it—your creativity, your income, your knowledge, your community. That's what actually changes outcomes.
