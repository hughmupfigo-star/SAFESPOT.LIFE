The facts, in order

A

A generation is being asked to build a future on ground that is actively being broken. SAFE is how we stop asking, and start building.

This is a direct response to a measurable failure of the societies that are meant to protect young people. The problem is structural. The solution has to be structural as well. Nothing below is rhetoric. All of it is auditable.

01

The world is at war with itself. Global military spending passed $2.4 trillion in 2023 (SIPRI). More than 110 armed conflicts are currently active (Geneva Academy). An estimated 468 million children are growing up in or near conflict zones (UNICEF). Every bomb and every bullet is a decision to fund destruction instead of development.

02

Youth are paying the bill. UK local authority spending on youth services was cut by roughly 70% between 2010 and 2022 (YMCA). Over 120,000 young people approach their council as homeless each year (Centrepoint). Knife-related offences involving under-18s in England and Wales remain near record levels. This is not a cultural problem. It is a withdrawal of resource.

03

We are one species on one planet. The borders are drawn. The flags are different. The biology is identical. An earth that is not at war with itself is not idealism — it is the only version of earth that adds up. SAFE will not fix geopolitics. SAFE will build the smallest unit that can — a community of young people given the tools to carry faith into the next day.

04

Prevention is cheaper than crisis. One custodial place for a young person costs the UK taxpayer roughly £250,000 a year (Ministry of Justice). A full SAFE hub serves thousands of young people a year for a fraction of that. The maths is not ideological. It is accounting.

05

One building. Seven rooms. Designed to scale. The blueprint is documented and replicable. UK first, then partnerships in other countries. Not a franchise. Not a charity empire. A model specific enough to work and simple enough to travel.

06

This is not optional. Every year the UK produces roughly 600,000 new sixteen-year-olds. Whatever we build for them, we build for all of us. Whatever we don't build for them, we pay for later — in policing, in hospitals, in prisons, in wasted potential. The cheque clears either way. The question is what it buys.
