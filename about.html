About — 004 · The Founder

Hugh Mupfigo.
Engineer. Chef. Builder.

Two careers, one discipline: precision under pressure. SAFE is what happens when civil engineering meets professional kitchens meets a decade of looking at a logo drawn in 2014 and finally understanding what it was for.

Why I Built SAFE

I spent years searching for a place to belong. Not in a sad way — but genuinely looking. In engineering offices, in kitchens, in care facilities. Each place taught me something about precision, about people, about what it means to build something that works. But I was always looking for something I couldn't find: a space that held all of me. Where I didn't have to make myself smaller to fit in. Where my weirdness wasn't a problem to solve but a strength to develop.

I realised this was true for so many young people I met. The ones who felt pushed out. The ones who'd been made to apologise for existing. The ones who couldn't find a room that had space for who they actually were. And I realised something else: we were all looking for the same thing.

SAFE exists because every young person deserves to be in a place built for them. Not a place that tolerates them. A place that actually needs them. That's why the energy in a SAFE space matters. We don't do toxic positivity — we do alignment. Rudeness, negativity, bad faith — those things don't belong here because they undermine the whole structure. They make people smaller. And that's the one thing we will not tolerate.

My team feels the same way. We're people who've felt misplaced. People who've had to rebuild ourselves. People who know what it looks like when a community cares more about maintaining comfort than about actually showing up for each other. We chose to build something different.

SAFE is a building. But it's also a promise. That if you've ever felt like you didn't belong anywhere, you belong here. That your voice matters. That your body, your mind, your identity, your vision for the future — all of it is taken seriously. The same seriousness we bring to engineering load calculations. To plating at service. To presence in care.

This is why I built it. This is why my team is here.
