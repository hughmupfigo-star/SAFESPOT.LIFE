Free Courses That Actually Build a Portfolio

You're spending 40 hours on "Intro to Computer Science" and nobody cares. Meanwhile, someone else spent 5 hours learning Zapier automation and got hired immediately. Here's what actually matters: micro-credentials in tools employers use every single day.

The Problem With Traditional Online Courses

You know the type. "Intro to Python." "Fundamentals of Web Development." They're £200 courses taught by people who haven't worked in tech in five years. You finish, get a certificate nobody looks at, and employers still ask: "What can you actually do?"

Here's the reality: employers don't care about your theoretical knowledge of algorithms. They care about whether you can solve their immediate problems. Can you automate their chaotic email system? Can you build a Notion database that doesn't make them want to scream? Can you write a prompt that actually works with AI?

Those skills take 5-20 hours to learn, not 100.

What Employers Actually Want (The Unsexy Truth)

Forget "full-stack developer" or "data analyst." What employers need are people who can:

  • Automate boring stuff (Zapier, Make, n8n)
  • Organise chaos (Notion, Airtable)
  • Talk to AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney prompting)
  • Wrangle data (Excel, Google Sheets, basic SQL)
  • Actually communicate (writing, presenting, explaining technical stuff to non-technical people)

Most job postings are for people with these exact skills. Most people don't have them. You can fix that in weeks, not years.

Micro-Credentials That Matter (All Free or Cheap)

1. Notion Mastery (The Gateway Drug to Every Job)

Notion Essentials & Beyond
YouTube: Thomas Frank, Notion official
Learn to build databases, dashboards, project management systems. This is actually impressive on a portfolio and most companies use Notion.
5-10 hours, completely free

Why it matters: Every job posting wants someone who can use Notion. Learn it → build 2-3 complex databases → screenshot them → add to portfolio → done.

Portfolio project: Build a "Student Portfolio System" in Notion: all your projects, learning log, goals, timeline. Screenshot and share the link. It's incredibly impressive.

2. Zapier Automation (The £10k/Year Skill)

Zapier Automation for Beginners
YouTube: Zapier official channel, TechLeadTV
Connect apps without code. Automate repetitive tasks. Save companies literally days of work every month.
3-5 hours, free

Why it matters: Companies pay consultants £1,000+ to do what you'll learn in 5 hours. If you can automate their lead collection, email follow-ups, or data logging, they'll hire you.

Portfolio project: Document 3 automations you built: "I automated our email follow-up system, saving 10 hours/week" with screenshots of the Zapier workflow and results.

3. Advanced Excel / Google Sheets (The Underrated Skill)

Excel for Productivity & Data Analysis
YouTube: KevinStratvert (phenomenal), Google Sheets official
VLOOKUP, pivot tables, data cleaning, formulas nobody knows about. This is what accountants, operations teams, and data analysts actually use.
8-15 hours, free

Why it matters: Most companies still run on spreadsheets. If you can turn chaos into clean data, you're immediately valuable.

Portfolio project: Create a complex spreadsheet: customer database with analysis, sales pipeline tracker, or budget model. Share as a downloadable template.

4. AI Prompting & Prompt Engineering (The Future Skill)

ChatGPT Mastery & Effective Prompting
OpenAI's guides, YouTube: Andrej Karpathy explanations
Not just "use ChatGPT." Learn to structure prompts that actually work. Learn about tokens, context, limitations, and getting exactly what you need.
2-5 hours initial, then practice

Why it matters: Everyone thinks they can use ChatGPT. Almost nobody actually knows how to use it effectively. You'll be in the 5%.

Portfolio project: Document "5 AI workflows I built for productivity": workflow diagrams, prompts, before/after time savings. Companies hire for this.

5. Airtable Mastery (The Notion Alternative)

Airtable Fundamentals
Airtable official tutorials, TechLeadTV
Database building without code. Automation, integrations, and workflows. Used by hundreds of startups.
6-10 hours, free

Why it matters: Some companies use Airtable instead of Notion. Know both = you're flexible and valuable.

6. Basic SQL (If You Want to Be Employable in Tech)

SQL in 100 Minutes
YouTube: Fireship, Khan Academy, Codecademy free tier
Query databases. Understand how data actually works. This is the language of data.
3-8 hours to start, practice ongoing

Why it matters: If you want any role near data or tech, SQL is non-negotiable. But you don't need to be an expert—just functional.

The Courses That Waste Your Time (What NOT to Do)

❌ "Intro to Computer Science" — Nobody hiring for your first job cares about CS fundamentals. They care if you can do their job.

❌ "Learn Python in 30 Days" — Unless you're pursuing a career in Python specifically, this is 30 hours you'll never get back.

❌ Certificates from Coursera for courses you didn't pay for — The "free" certificates with nothing credential value. Skip them.

❌ "Full-Stack Web Development Bootcamp" — Unless you're already sure you want to code full-time, this is probably not the time investment.

❌ Anything that takes 100+ hours and doesn't result in a tangible portfolio project.

How to Actually Build a Portfolio (In Real Time)

Step 1: Pick 3 tools from the list above. (I'd recommend Notion, Zapier, and ChatGPT prompting.)

Step 2: Do the courses. 2-3 weeks, maybe 5 hours a week.

Step 3: Build 1 project in each tool. Something real. Something you'd actually use or that solves a real problem.

Step 4: Document it. Screenshots, video walkthrough, before/after metrics. Write: "I automated X and saved Y hours/money."

Step 5: Create a portfolio site. One page on your website: "Tools I've mastered" with projects and results.

Step 6: When you apply for jobs, send them your portfolio. "I've built X in Notion, automated Y with Zapier, and here's my work." That's it.

Real-World Example: Actually Landing a Job

Let's say you're 18, want to make money, don't have professional experience.

Week 1-2: Learn Notion (6 hours). Build a portfolio system in Notion.

Week 3: Learn Zapier (3 hours). Build an automation for lead email follow-ups.

Week 4: Learn ChatGPT prompting (2 hours). Document 3 AI workflows you built.

Week 5: Create a one-page portfolio site. Screenshot your work. Write: "I help small businesses automate, organise, and use AI effectively."

Week 6 onwards: Email 5-10 local businesses: "I noticed you're [struggling with X / could benefit from Y]. I automate stuff like this. Here's my portfolio."

You'll get meetings. Some will hire you. Estimate: £300-1,000 for a one-off project, or £2,000-5,000 for ongoing part-time work.

Where to Actually Learn (Free or Cheap)

  • YouTube: Thomas Frank, Fireship, TechLeadTV, official channel tutorials. Start here, it's free.
  • Official tutorials: Notion, Zapier, Airtable, Google all have official free guides. Use them.
  • Skillshare (free trial): 1 month free, cancel after. Tons of micro-credential courses.
  • Udemy sale courses: Wait for sales (always on), £10-15 for specific courses. Still cheap compared to bootcamps.
  • Practice projects: Don't just watch. Build. That's where learning actually happens.

The Bottom Line

Stop spending weeks learning broad concepts. Spend 5-20 hours learning exactly what employers need. Build 2-3 projects. Document them. Share your work. You'll be more employable than people who spent months on theoretical courses.

Specificity beats breadth. Results beat credentials.
