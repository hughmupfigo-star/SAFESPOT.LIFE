The Hub — 002 · One building, seven rooms

One building.
Seven rooms.
Designed to scale.

The SAFE Hub is a holistic youth development facility. Every room addresses a specific human need — body, mind, voice, identity, knowledge, stillness, nourishment — and every room connects to every other. Below are the concept visualisations and the masterplan for the first build in the UK.

The Rooms

B

01 · Alchemist's Kitchen

Botanical Apothecary Kitchen & Clinic

A teaching kitchen and nutritional clinic in one room. Food as the first language of safety — and as medicine. Young people learn to cook, prep and understand what enters the body as both fuel and regulation.

02 · Warrior Dojo

Martial Arts Dojo — Discipline & Self-Regulation

A dojo is not a gym. It is a structured environment for converting aggression into precision, impulse into discipline, and fear into presence. Core to the SAFE physical curriculum.

03 · Kinetic Lab

Kinetic Movement Lab — Skate & Parkour

Controlled risk. Flow state. Public skate and parkour structures, indoors, year-round. Physical literacy through environments young people actually want to spend time in.

04 · Silence Sanctuary

Meditation Room

The programme that makes all the others possible. Breathwork, stillness, guided practice. A room where doing nothing is the curriculum.

05 · Resonance Booth

Music Production & Recording Studio

A working studio. Not a youth club setup — professional grade equipment in a room designed to tell a young person that their voice is worth capturing properly.

06 · Identity Studio

Fashion & Identity Lab

Confidence and personal development through dress, construction and self-presentation. The question is not "what do you wear" — it is "who are you when nobody is telling you".

06b · Expression Studio

Painting & Expression Through Art

Canvas, brush, colour, silence. A room where young people put what they cannot yet say into paint. Expression as release, expression as evidence. The image on the wall is a record of the day they chose to make something instead of carry it.

07 · Architect's Library

Tech & Learning Centre

Books, devices, bandwidth, mentors. Knowledge is not the problem. Access is. This room closes the access gap.

Outdoor · Carpentry

Carpentry Pavilion

Outdoor, open-sided. Timber, tools and craft. Making something with your hands is one of the fastest ways to tell yourself you are not powerless.

Outdoor · Gardening

Gardening Zone

Food grown on site feeds the kitchen. Soil, seed, patience — all of it transferable to how a young person eventually grows themselves.

Outdoor · Basketball

Outdoor Basketball Court

The front door of the building. Open, visible, rules-based, community-owned. Any young person in the neighbourhood is welcome — a SAFE member or not.
