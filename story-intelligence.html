Mental Health

Burnout is hitting younger every year

Stories from 14–17 year-olds describe symptoms once associated with overworked adults: chronic fatigue, emotional numbness, inability to experience joy. School systems and social media are cited most often.

Poverty & Economics

Youth unemployment is reshaping futures

Across Europe and South America, submitters describe qualifying for jobs they can't afford to take—entry-level roles that don't cover transport, housing, or food in the cities where the work exists.

41 stories from 12 countries this month

Community

Third places are disappearing

Libraries, youth clubs, and community centres that young people relied on are closing. What's left? The mall. The phone. Nowhere that doesn't cost money or screen time to exist in.

Mentioned in 28 stories across 9 countries

Who this report serves

Intelligence that helps the people who need to act most

Youth voices are gathered. Patterns are surfaced. What happens next depends on who reads it. Story Intelligence is designed to be genuinely useful to the organisations, institutions, and individuals with the power to make change—not just to watch it.

🏛️

Department of Education

Unfiltered data on what's actually happening inside classrooms, schools, and the lives of students—before it becomes a crisis that lands on a minister's desk. Useful for curriculum review, pastoral policy, and early intervention strategy.

🗣️

Youth Councils

Evidence that young people can bring to the table. Story Intelligence gives youth representatives a structured, credible dataset to support advocacy—not just anecdote. Stronger voice. Harder to ignore.

🤝

NGOs — Barnardo's, Save the Children & others

Real-time intelligence on where need is highest. Useful for grant applications, programme design, and directing resources toward emerging issues before they're mainstream. Data direct from the young people these organisations exist to serve.

🔬

University Researchers

A qualitative dataset of youth narratives spanning geography, age, and topic—updated monthly. Available as primary source material for social science, public health, education, and media studies research.

📰

Media Outlets

Story leads that exist nowhere else. Journalists covering youth, education, or social affairs can use Story Intelligence to identify underreported issues, find real voices, and get ahead of the stories that matter before they break.

🏘️

Local Government

Granular, community-level insight. Councils can see what young people in their area are actually experiencing—separate from national trends—and make better decisions on services, spaces, and youth programme funding.

📚

Schools — as a Research Tool

For teachers, pastoral leads, and school leaders who want to understand what's happening in students' lives beyond the school gate. Useful for PSHE planning, safeguarding awareness, and informing staff development.

The same data that shows a mental health crisis in 14-year-olds in Bristol can inform a local authority budget decision, a Barnardo's programme launch, a university research paper, and a Guardian feature—simultaneously. That's what makes it worth collecting.
