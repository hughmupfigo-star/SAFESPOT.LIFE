Who we are

We believe prevention is cheaper than aftermath.

SAFE is building infrastructure for young people. Not for the ones who fit into existing systems. For the ones sleeping rough. For the ones in crisis. For the ones everyone else has given up on.

We do this three ways: a platform where young people can access resources and community. A portable studio that brings music production, meditation, and art to shelters and community centres. And partnerships with the people already doing this work—shelter staff, community leaders, people with lived experience.

Every sale in our store funds the build. Every person who joins us is choosing to work on something that stops people from falling through the cracks. That's the team we're building.

About these roles: We're UK-based but open to remote globally. We move fast, we're mission-driven, and we don't waste time on performative stuff. You'll own what you build. You'll talk to young people, understand what they need, and iterate. Full-time positions with competitive pay, flexible hours, and the kind of equity that means something when we scale.

Why join us

You'll work on something that matters.

Every role here exists to serve one thing: young people who've been told they don't matter. You'll build product for people experiencing homelessness. You'll partner with people who've survived crisis. You'll create content that shifts how systems work. You'll do work that prevents tragedy instead of responding to it.

We move fast. We're not building bureaucracy. We iterate based on feedback from young people, not strategy documents. We ship, we measure, we change.

We're transparent about what we don't know. We're a new organisation. We'll figure things out together. We need people who are comfortable with ambiguity and excited by the possibility of shaping how this grows.

How to apply

To apply, send the following to careers@safespot.life:

  • Your CV or portfolio link – Show us your work. What have you built? What have you written? What have you designed or shipped?
  • A cover letter explaining why you want to join SAFE – This is required. Tell us: What about SAFE's mission resonates with you? Why do you want to do this work? What would you bring to this team?
  • Anything else you think we should know – Previous projects, articles, design work, community involvement, whatever shows us who you are.

We're not looking for perfect experience. We're looking for people who believe in this and know how to show their work. Your cover letter matters as much as your portfolio—we need to understand why you're here, not just what you've done.

We'll review applications as they come in. If you're a fit, we'll talk. We'll ask about your experience, but we'll also ask about who you are and what you believe.

We're committed to building a team that reflects the diversity of young people we serve. We actively encourage applications from people of colour, disabled people, LGBTQ+ people, and people with lived experience of the systems we're trying to change.
