General Questions

If you have questions about SAFE, our mission, or how to get involved, please reach out directly.

What is SAFE?
SAFE is a permanent foundation building holistic youth development hubs for at-risk young people. It's designed around seven core environments — kitchen, dojo, kinetic lab, meditation space, recording studio, identity studio, and learning centre — all in one building. Learn more on the Mission page.
What are the seven rooms?
Each room addresses a specific human need: the kitchen teaches nourishment and body care, the dojo builds discipline and self-regulation, the kinetic lab develops physical literacy, meditation provides stillness, the recording studio enables creative voice, the identity studio develops self-confidence, and the learning centre closes the knowledge access gap. See detailed descriptions on The Hub page.
Why does SAFE exist?
Youth services have been cut by 70% since 2010. Young people face unprecedented challenges — homelessness, violence, mental health crises. SAFE exists because prevention is cheaper than crisis. Read the full context and statistics on the Mission page.
Where is SAFE being built?
The vision is a SAFE hub in every country on earth — wherever and whenever the plans are permitted. Locations and timelines are TBA as we navigate partnerships, permissions, and local requirements. The blueprint is ready; the world is next.

You can support SAFE by sharing the mission, becoming a partner, making a donation, or getting directly involved. Contact us at enquiries@safespot.life to explore how you can contribute.

Yes. Visit our Shop to browse hoodies, t-shirts, caps, mugs, posters, and more. All proceeds support the SAFE mission.
Who is behind SAFE?
SAFE was founded by Hugh Mupfigo, a former civil engineer and professional chef who has lived through crisis, healed, and spent years learning what actually works. He's built this framework because he's been where young people are—and he knows there's a better path. Learn more about Hugh and the vision on the About page.
