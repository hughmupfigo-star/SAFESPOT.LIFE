What we look for

Does your book serve young people? Does it tell truth about systems, identity, prevention, healing, or real education? We want to read it.

Non-fiction

Books grounded in reality, research, and lived experience. Not theory divorced from how systems actually work.

Youth-centred

Written WITH young people in mind. Not AT them. Books they'll actually read and understand.

Truth-focused

Books that unmask systems. That show what's hidden. That help young people understand the architecture they're inside.

Systems-aware

Books about identity, prevention, healing, power, relationships, media, money. Books that name how systems shape us.

Pathways, not just problems

You don't just show what's broken. You show how young people move through it differently. Prevention, not just crisis response.

Independent voice

Books that see what mainstream publishing misses. Ideas that matter. Writing that serves people, not algorithms.

How it works

Three steps from submission to reader.

01

You submit

Send us your manuscript. Non-fiction. Youth-centred. Truth-focused. Systems-aware. That's the brief. We'll read it.

02

We read

We read everything. We're looking for clarity of thinking, writing that serves young people, content that unmasks systems and shows pathways through them. We'll get back to you within 4 weeks.

03

You earn

If we love it, we create a payment link. You set the price. We distribute it. You keep 100% of proceeds. We get to build a library of voices that matter.

Why we publish

We value truth in good writing.

Mainstream publishing serves profit first. We serve young people first. If a book helps them understand systems, prevent crisis, heal, or see themselves clearly—it belongs in the world. That's not a business decision. That's a mission decision.

We take 0% of proceeds because we're not trying to monetise truth. We're trying to distribute it. We're building proof that good authors and honest writing and young people in the centre can exist together. That's how we add to SAFE's mission: one author, one book, one young person at a time.
