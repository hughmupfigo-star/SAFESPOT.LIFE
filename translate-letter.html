Translate this letter.

Got an official letter you've been scared to open — from a council, a landlord, the Job Centre, a school, a debt collector, a court? Take a photo, upload a PDF, or paste the text. We'll read it and explain it in plain English — or your own language — pull out the dates, tell you what to do, and who to call for free help.

Private & safe. Nothing you paste or upload is saved, logged, or published anywhere. It's read once to explain it, then forgotten.

Share only what you need — you can cover or cross out your full name, address, and any reference numbers first. The explanation still works without them. Please don't upload letters or documents that aren't yours to share.

Which letters to be extra careful with

Take extra care before uploading: immigration or asylum papers, anything with a case number or National Insurance number, medical or mental-health letters, and anything that names other people. If you're unsure, paste just the part you don't understand. In danger right now? Call 999. Under 18 and need to talk? Childline 0800 1111.

This is information, not legal advice. SAFE's Letter Translator helps you understand what a letter is saying in plain English. For anything that could affect your housing, money, or legal status, talk to a qualified human: Citizens Advice (0800 144 8848), Shelter (0808 800 4444), StepChange for debt (0800 138 1111), or LawWorks for a free legal clinic. If you are under 18 and in crisis, call Childline (0800 1111). Letters are sent to our server, read by Claude (Anthropic) to produce the explanation, and then forgotten — we don't store, log, or train on what you send.
